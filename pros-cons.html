Pros & Cons

Any usable energy produced by water, whether it originates from turbines, dams, or some other source, is known as hydropower. Hydropower, like all energy sources, whether renewable or not, has advantages and disadvantages. We'll go through some of the main advantages and disadvantages of hydropower technology.


Pros::


1. The use of hydropower is clean and renewable.


Water is a clean energy source, unlike conventional fossil fuels, which discharge hazardous pollutants into the air and water. Additionally, since hydroelectric facilities don't consume water while they are in operation, hydropower is a completely renewable source of electricity. Hydropower will always be a practical option to produce electricity because of how the water cycle operates naturally.


2. Irrigation can be powered by hydropower.


In addition to producing energy, hydropower plants have the significant advantage of being able to supply a source of irrigation for nearby crops. Hydropower reservoirs can be used as a safe and dependable source of freshwater, particularly in regions where rain is infrequent and droughts are frequent.


3. In the long term, hydropower is affordable.


Although hydropower has large upfront expenditures, it has been found to be one of the most affordable sources of renewable energy over time. Once the necessary infrastructure has been built, hydropower requires less upkeep than other energy sources, and modifications are being made to enhance its performance.



CONS::


1. It Has an Effect on the Environment


The potential negative effects of hydroelectric electricity on the environment may be its biggest drawback. Water flow obstructions can also have a significant impact on fish migration, particularly for species like salmon that depend on rivers for spawning. Even the biological signals that instruct fish where to go when it's time to migrate might be impacted by dams.


2.There are dry spells.


Water may and does experience cycles of excess and scarcity, which should be considered while assessing the benefits and drawbacks of hydroelectric power. The production of energy can be significantly impacted by lower-than-normal water levels, which is a drawback of hydroelectric power.


Hydro energy production can contribute to drought conditions downstream if it doesn't allow enough water to get through, in addition to being affected by drought.


3. It Is Not Often Safe


Dams still pose risks notwithstanding the absence of combustible fuel. Both dam failure and construction mishaps have the potential to cause harm or even fatalities.
