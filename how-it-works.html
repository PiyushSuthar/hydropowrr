HOW EXACTLY DO HYDROPOWER PLANTS CREATE ELECTRICITY?

So how precisely do we produce power from water? Actually, the processes used by coal-fired and hydroelectric power plants to generate electricity are similar. In both instances, a power source is used to drive a turbine, which is a device like a propeller and which turns a metal shaft in an electric generator, the motor that generates energy. While a hydroelectric facility employs falling water to operate the turbine, a coal-fired power station uses steam to turn the turbine blades. The outcomes are identical.
